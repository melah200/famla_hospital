    About Us

    Hospital Famla de Bafoussam is a relatively small health center in the city Bafoussam in the west of Cameroon. Between 100 and 150 meet there every day Patient a. In addition to general medicine, the hospital also offers services like cardiology, dentistry, dermatology, the antenatal visit as well as the birth...

    little story about our center and its particularity.

    famla hospital located in the western Cameroon district. more precisely in the west of Cameroon is a small health center located not far from carefour biao, between the hotel golden center and the sky garden.

    • The famla hospital is made up of a multi-storey building. the floors are distributed to the resort. the center has a large free space for visitors, a large courtyard with a beautiful garden. Despite its extension in 1950, the first buildings still exist, like the entrance pavilion which bears the date 1940. The emphasis is on its aesthetic side, with a combination of several colors on the various facades. the hospital remains this during a major project in order to obtain more room.

    It was originally reserved for indigenous patients, who were then prohibited from entering the general hospital. The native hospital of bafoussam was then subjected to the regime of the indigénat by the French administration. It is renamed famla hospital [When?], Named after its chief doctor, who twice stayed in Cameroon. During his first stay, from 1936 to 1938, the doctor-captain Jean famla, rubbed shoulders with Dr. Louis-Paul Aujoulat, who created the Ad Lucem Medical Foundation in 1936 in Cameroon. His second stay, in 1940, was short-lived and marked by the reception, on August 27, 1940, of Commander Leclerc, envoy of General de Gaulle. He left Cameroon to accompany the 1st Cameroon infantry regiment to which he was assigned. He died in the countryside in 1941.

    Cardiology

    It provides care for cardiovascular pathologies (myocardial infarction, heart failure, arrhythmias, etc.) and develops the intervention sector. Interventional cardiology: in emergency or in scheduled hospitalization, 3 cardiologists carry out coronary angiograms and coronary angioplasties, in particular with stent placement. Rhythmology: 3 practitioners ensure the treatment of rhythm disorders (by drugs or radiofrequency ablation), the installation of cardiac pacemakers, defibrillators. The heart failure and functional explorations sector is taken care of by several hospital practitioners. They provide treatment for patients with heart disease and perform a number of examinations (cardiac ultrasound, stress test, stress ultrasound, etc.) The Cardiology Intensive Care Unit welcomes patients with the most severe pathologies for close care.

    Neurology

    Patients are received for diagnostic assessments and / or for therapeutic care. The department has areas of consultation (with emergency consultations), day hospitalization, week hospitalization and traditional hospitalization. In addition to expertise in the various fields of neurology, one of the specificities of the department is its resuscitation sector dedicated to neurological patients. The department is also backed by several national centers of reference or competence

    Hepatology

    Hepatology is one of the specialties of gastroenterology treating the medical management of hepatic diseases of the liver and bile ducts. There are many causes of liver disease: medication, alcohol, drugs, many viruses, genetic abnormalities, diabetes, malignant and / or benign tumors, etc. After a first diagnosis made by his attending physician, the patient is received in outpatient consultation by the hepatologist at the Sion site. Depending on the category of the pathology, several analyzes can be carried out in order to determine the degree of severity of the disease and to define indications and therapeutic treatment. These analyzes are performed using blood tests, urine tests, ultrasound imaging (CT) and biopsies.

    Pediatrics

    Pediatrics is the medical specialty that deals with the diagnosis and treatment of diseases in newborns, children and adolescents. According to Article 1 of the Convention on the Rights of the Child, this group includes all people up to the age of 18. The pediatrician is the specialist in pediatric and adolescent medicine. One of its main tasks is early diagnosis and therapy, which is critical to the prognosis of a developing child.

    General Medicine

    General medicine, or family medicine, is a medical specialty1 supporting the sustainable monitoring, well-being and general primary health care of a community, without being limited to groups of diseases relating to an organ, 'an age, or a particular sex. The general practitioner, also called general practitioner or family doctor, is therefore often consulted to diagnose symptoms before treating the disease or referring the patient to another specialist
